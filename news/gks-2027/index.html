GKS 2027: Korea's fully funded scholarship opens in September — embassy track first

deadline 15 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts

  • Embassy track opens: ~September 2026 · university track: ~October 2026
  • Covers: full tuition + ₩1,070,000–1,140,000/month + flights + 1 year of Korean
  • Academic bar: roughly top 20% of your class (or ~80% grade average)
  • All applications go through the Study in Korea portal — one track per cycle

The Global Korea Scholarship (GKS) — the Korean government's flagship full ride — runs on a fixed annual clock: the undergraduate cycle for 2027 entry opens its embassy track around September 2026 and its university track around October 2026, with results in January and arrival in Korea in late February 2027.

What you actually get

  • 100% tuition (up to ₩5,000,000 per semester) — admission fee waived
  • One free year of intensive Korean language before the degree
  • Monthly stipend: ₩1,070,000 during the language year, ₩1,140,000 during the degree
  • Round-trip airfare, ₩200,000 settlement allowance, health insurance

Embassy track vs university track — pick one

You can apply through one track only per cycle. The embassy track (via the Korean Embassy in Tashkent) lets you list several universities and generally has more country slots for Uzbekistan; the university track means applying directly to one partner university. Since 2026 everything is submitted digitally through the Study in Korea portal — no more paper packages.

Where English fits

GKS does not hard-require IELTS — but a solid English certificate is one of the few concrete ways to stand out in the document round, and several partner universities award bonus points for it. If you're preparing over the summer, an IELTS taken by August lands in time for the September window.
