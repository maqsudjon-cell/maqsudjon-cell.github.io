Erasmus Mundus 2027: fully funded master's across Europe — deadlines start this autumn

deadline 15 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts

  • Applications for 2027 entry open October–December 2026
  • Deadlines are per programme — mostly November 2026 – February 2027
  • You study in 2–3 European countries and get one joint degree
  • Funding: full tuition + monthly allowance + travel, paid by the EU

Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters is the EU's "study in several countries on one scholarship" programme: each master's is run by a consortium of universities in two or three countries, and scholarship students move between them with tuition covered and a monthly allowance on top. For 2027 entry, application portals open this autumn.

The thing everyone gets wrong

There is no single Erasmus Mundus application and no single deadline. There's a catalogue of separate programmes, each with its own website, requirements and cut-off date — mostly falling between November and February. The official catalogue lives on the EU's Erasmus+ site; pick programmes there, not from Telegram screenshots.

Your summer-to-autumn plan

  1. Shortlist 3+ programmes now. You can apply to multiple consortia in the same season — that multiplies your chances.
  2. Check each programme's English requirement. Most ask IELTS 6.5+ (some 6.0, some 7.0). Your certificate must be ready when you submit — an autumn IELTS is the deadline that actually matters.
  3. Recommendation letters: two academic referees, warned in September, deliver in November. Warned in December, they deliver stress.
  4. Motivation letter per programme — consortia can tell a copy-paste letter instantly.
