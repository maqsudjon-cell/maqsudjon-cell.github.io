El-Yurt Umidi 2026: 500 quotas in the first call — how to be ready for the next one

deadline 15 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts — first 2026 call

  • Application window: 3–24 April 2026 (closed)
  • Quotas: 500 — 167 bachelor's · 232 master's · 38 PhD (plus reserve places)
  • Requirement: acceptance letter from a top-ranked university (QS / THE lists)
  • Next call: announced at eyuf.uz — historically more than one call per year

El-Yurt Umidi — the Presidential foundation that fully funds Uzbek citizens' studies at the world's top universities — ran its first 2026 open call in April: 500 quotas covering bachelor's, master's and doctoral study abroad. Applications went through the foundation's online platform, and for the first time final-year students without a diploma in hand were also allowed to apply.

The pattern that matters

El-Yurt Umidi has been running multiple calls per year — a second selection typically follows the first within months. If you missed April, the realistic move is not waiting passively: everything on the checklist below takes weeks to months, and the application window is usually only about three weeks long.

The checklist for the next call

  1. Admission first. The foundation funds people who already hold an acceptance letter from a top-ranking university (QS/THE). University applications themselves take 2–6 months — this is the real deadline, not the foundation's.
  2. IELTS. Top-300 programmes typically ask for 6.5–7.5. If your target course wants 7.0 and you're at 6.0, that's roughly 3–4 months of focused work.
  3. Documents. Transcript, diploma (or enrollment proof for final-year students), recommendation letters, motivation letter — collect them before the call opens, not during.
  4. Watch eyuf.uz and the foundation's official channels — calls open with short notice.
