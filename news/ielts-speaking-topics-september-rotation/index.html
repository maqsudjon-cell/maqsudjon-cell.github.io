IELTS Speaking topics rotate in September — use the current bank while it lasts

exam update 16 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts

  • Speaking topic pool partially refreshes about every 4 months (Jan / May / Sep)
  • Exam in August? The current May–August bank still applies
  • Exam in October? Expect part of the topics to be new
  • Rotation affects Part 1, 2 and 3 topics — not the format

IELTS Speaking works from a topic pool that partially rotates on a roughly four-month rhythm — around January, May and September. Prep centres publish "current topics" lists each cycle; they stay useful until the next swap.

How to use this

  1. Exam before September: practising the current May–August topic lists is high-value — the pool is stable.
  2. Exam in autumn: don't memorise answers to specific topics. Build transferable material instead: 8–10 flexible stories (a person, a place, a decision, a difficulty) that adapt to almost any Part 2 card.
  3. Either way, examiners reward fluency and development, not topic prediction.
