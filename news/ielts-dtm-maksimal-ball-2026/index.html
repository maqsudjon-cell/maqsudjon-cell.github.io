IELTS 5.5+ gives you the maximum foreign-language score at DTM — the 2026 rules

exam update 15 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts

  • B2 certificate (IELTS 5.5+) = maximum foreign-language score, no exam needed
  • Worth 93.0 points if the language is your first subject, 63.0 if second
  • New for 2026: B1 certificate = 75% of the maximum score
  • Certificate must be valid (not expired) on submission day — IELTS lasts 2 years

For hundreds of thousands of abituriyents sitting Uzbekistan's university entrance exams this summer, one rule quietly decides more points than any single test day: a valid B2-level foreign-language certificate — which for English means IELTS 5.5 or higher — replaces the foreign-language exam with the maximum score.

What it's worth in real numbers

  • If English is your first (main) subject: the certificate banks 93.0 points — the full block.
  • If English is your second subject: 63.0 points, guaranteed.
  • You skip the foreign-language section entirely — less exam-day risk, more time for the other subjects.

The 2026 change: B1 now counts too

From the 2026 admission season, applicants holding a valid B1 national or international certificate get 75% of the maximum foreign-language score on programmes where the language sits in the subject block. It's a real consolation prize — but the gap between 75% and 100% is exactly the gap between IELTS ~4.5–5.0 and 5.5, which for most students is a few months of focused practice.

Three traps to avoid

  1. Expiry. IELTS is valid for 2 years from the test date. If it expires before document submission, it counts for nothing.
  2. Cutting it too close. Computer-delivered results take up to ~5 days, paper up to ~13. Book with a safety margin before the admission window.
  3. Aiming at exactly 5.5. If your practice mocks say 5.5, exam-day variance can drop you to 5.0. Train until mocks show 6.0 — then 5.5 is your floor, not your ceiling.
