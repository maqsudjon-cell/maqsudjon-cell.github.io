Your IELTS expires in 2 years — plan backwards from the application, not the exam

exam update 16 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts

  • TRF validity: 2 years from the test date
  • Certificate must be valid on the day you submit — DTM, scholarships, universities all check
  • Too early = expires mid-journey; too late = results miss the deadline
  • Sweet spot for most plans: 6–12 months before the application window

An IELTS result lives exactly two years. That single fact quietly kills applications in both directions: students who take the exam in school and find it expired by the time they apply abroad, and students who book it two weeks before a deadline and watch the results arrive late.

Plan backwards

  1. Write down the application window (university, scholarship, or DTM submission — see the DTM rules).
  2. Subtract result-processing time (1–5 days computer, ~13 paper) plus one retake's worth of margin.
  3. Check the certificate still covers the entire process — some universities want it valid at enrolment, not just application.
