IELTS UKVI vs Academic — the expensive mix-up UK applicants keep making

exam update 16 July 2026 · by Maqsudjon Polatov

Key facts

  • Same test, same difficulty — UKVI just runs under extra UK Home Office rules
  • Degree-level students at licensed universities usually don't need UKVI — the regular Academic often suffices
  • Below-degree courses, foundation and some visa routes do require UKVI
  • Wrong version = book and pay again — always confirm with your university first

IELTS for UKVI is not a harder exam — it's the same Academic or General Training test delivered under UK-government-approved conditions, and it costs more. The trap: booking UKVI "just in case", or booking regular IELTS when your route legally requires UKVI.

The rule of thumb

  1. Degree course at a licensed UK university (bachelor's/master's): universities can accept the regular Academic test — most do. Ask your admissions office before paying.
  2. Foundation, pathway, below-degree courses and certain visa categories: UKVI version required.
  3. The authoritative list of approved tests lives on gov.uk — check it, not Telegram chats.
